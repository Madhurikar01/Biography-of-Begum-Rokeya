Begum Rokeya Sakhawat Hossain

A Trailblazer in Women's Rights and Education

Biography

Rokeya Sakhawat Hossain (9 December 1880 – 9 December 1932), commonly known as Begum Rokeya, was a renowned Bengali feminist thinker, writer, educator, and political activist from British India. She is celebrated as a pioneer of women's liberation in Bangladesh and India. Her advocacy for gender equality and education revolutionized the way women were perceived and educated in the region.

Begum Rokeya emphasized the importance of education for women's empowerment, founding the first school aimed primarily at Muslim girls in Kolkata. Despite facing significant criticism and obstacles, she remained committed to her cause throughout her life. She also founded the Muslim Women's Association in 1916 and was actively involved in various conferences and debates about women's rights until her death.

Social and Political Activism

In 1916, Begum Rokeya founded the Muslim Women's Association, an organization committed to enhancing women's access to education and improving employment prospects for Muslim women in particular. Her leadership came to the forefront in 1926 when she presided over the Bengal Women's Education Conference in Kolkata, a landmark event that sought to galvanize support for women's education across communities. This conference marked a significant milestone in her efforts to unify women in advocating for their right to education and greater societal participation.

Rokeya's involvement in intellectual debates and public forums continued throughout her life, as she consistently used her platform to challenge societal norms and promote gender equality. Her tireless advocacy in debates, conferences, and written works not only advanced women's rights in education but also paved the way for future generations to break through cultural barriers. Even up until her passing in 1932, she remained a leading voice in the movement for women's advancement in British India.

Legacy and Awards

While Rokeya did not receive formal awards during her lifetime, several honors have been bestowed posthumously in recognition of her significant contributions:

  • Rokeya Padak (1971): Instituted by Bangladesh, this prestigious award recognizes women who have made outstanding contributions to society, mirroring Begum Rokeya’s work for women's rights and social justice.
  • UNESCO Commemoration (2004): In honor of her contributions to women's education and equality, UNESCO recognized Begum Rokeya as a global figure in promoting gender equity.
  • Posthumous Bengal Academy Award (1995): Awarded by the Bengal Academy for her remarkable influence on education and social reform in the region.
  • Google Doodle (2017): On December 9, 2017, Google created a doodle to commemorate Begum Rokeya’s 137th birthday, celebrating her influence on gender equality and education.

Eponyms in Honor of Begum Rokeya

These eponyms reflect the profound and lasting impact of Begum Rokeya’s contributions to education, women's rights, and social reform:

  • Begum Rokeya Day: Celebrated annually on December 9, this day commemorates Begum Rokeya's birth and acknowledges her significant contributions to women's rights and education in Bangladesh. It serves as a national occasion to reflect on her enduring legacy and promote ongoing efforts towards gender equality and women's empowerment.
  • Begum Rokeya Padak (1971): Established in 1971 by Bangladesh, the Begum Rokeya Padak is a prestigious award that honors women who have made remarkable contributions to society, particularly in the fields of women's rights, social justice, and community development. The award pays tribute to Begum Rokeya’s lifelong commitment to advancing women's status and her groundbreaking work in promoting gender equality.
  • Begum Rokeya University (2008): Located in Rangpur, Bangladesh, Begum Rokeya University is a public state institution named in her honor. The university was established to enhance higher education and research opportunities, continuing Begum Rokeya’s legacy of advocating for educational reforms. It aims to empower students through knowledge and innovation, reflecting her vision of education as a tool for societal progress.
  • Rokeya Hall: Rokeya Hall is the largest female residential hall at the University of Dhaka, dedicated to Begum Rokeya’s commitment to women's education. The hall provides accommodation for female students and serves as a symbol of her significant impact on advancing gender equality in education. It stands as a tribute to her pioneering efforts in promoting women's higher education.
  • Begum Rokeya Memorial Center: Situated in Pairaband, Bangladesh, the Begum Rokeya Memorial Center functions as an academic and cultural hub dedicated to preserving and promoting her legacy. The center organizes events, exhibitions, and educational programs that highlight her contributions to women's rights and social reform, ensuring that her influence continues to inspire future generations.
  • Rokeya Shoroni: A road in Dhaka named Rokeya Shoroni honors Begum Rokeya’s profound impact on society and her advocacy for social reform. The road serves as a physical reminder of her contributions to social justice and gender equality, embedding her legacy into the urban landscape of Dhaka.
  • Sakhawat Memorial Govt. Girls' High School: Located in Kolkata, West Bengal, this school is named in memory of Begum Rokeya’s late husband, Sakhawat Hossain. It reflects her educational ideals and commitment to empowering girls through education. The school continues her mission by providing quality education to young women, fostering their academic and personal development.
  • Begum Rokeya Smriti Balika Vidalaya: This school in Saltlake, West Bengal, is named in honor of Begum Rokeya to perpetuate her work in women’s education and empowerment. The institution aims to uphold her vision of educational access and equality for girls, offering opportunities for learning and growth that echo her pioneering efforts in advocating for women's rights.
